This is part of my “Realign 2020” series. In it, I document my process during a content and design realign of this site. I’ll update the site piece-by-piece as I post. The design and content will evolve through the series and beyond.

Going into this, I didn’t know exactly what colors I wanted, but I knew I wanted to use a lot of them. I wanted my color usage to come across as deliberate, but I also wanted to leave the door open for future additions. With this update, I have a design where no hue, shade, nor tint is off limits.

In this interation I’ve added a light color scheme and selector. Along the way, accessiblity was a guiding principle. I’ve put a lot effort into making sure the colors meet WCAG contrast ratios. It’s not perfect, but moving in the right direction.

A working combination

I started with what I had in place. A dark grey background #292929, not-quite-white text #f8f8f8, and a pink accent color #ff607f.

I’ve used the dark grey and offwhite on this site since 2018. I’ve played with the accent color over the years. The original was a muted green. Then maybe a purple. I changed it every few months for fun.

The color usage felt a bit trapped with only a single accent color. That's an early thing I worked on for this. I still wanted a primary accent color to highlight links, but I wanted at least one more accent color for embellishment.

My process for picking color combinations is definitely not a science. It’s slow and tedious trail and error. Pick a color. Pick another. See if they work together. Repeat. The first color I got excited about was a new accent color for the dark scheme, #00eb9b.

To get to this color I started with emerald green. I didn’t have a hex for it, I just did a image search for “emerald green” and picked the one I thought looked the best. The reason I started with that color? My hair was sorta emerald green/blue at the time. See what I mean by not a science?

From there I checked contrast with my dark scheme background color #292929 using the WebAIM contrast checker. I’d use this green for text links so I needed it to have a ratio that met at least WCAG AA for normal text. Using contrast as a guidline for choosing colors like this is super helpful. Choosing colors for a design is a wide-open process, so anything that helps narrow the scope is welcome.
